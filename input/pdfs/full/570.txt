Summary
We present a clustering method using dynamical and statistical analysis. It is a data mining
tool which works on multiple time series to detect meaningfull states. The aim of this unsuper-
vised method is to find relevant information within the time series. It is based on the detection
of singularities of the time series and on the analysis of their correlation factors in intervals
defined by these singularities. For the application the time series are biochemical signals mea-
sured during a bioprocess. This approach is used to confirm the knowledge of experts without
using the a priori knowledge of these experts. It is used for the search of physiological states
in a fed-batch process.

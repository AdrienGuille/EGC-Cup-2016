Summary
The human brain provides typical complex data for data mining. Extracting active
voxels from brain images is often very dicult due to a very high level of various noises.
First experiments of current data mining algorithms in this domain showed their low
performances and recognition abilities. In this article, we present a new interactive,
data-driven, approach to mine fMRI images in order to show how the brain works from
fMRI data. Several unsupervised classication algorithms are compared on 3D image
sequences from fMRI, reporting results with respect to the number of classes, the noise
level, and the ratio of the activated/observed areas.

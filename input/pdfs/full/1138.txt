A Temporal Representation of 
Point-Interval Relations
Björn Gottfried and Hanna Bauerdick
The Problem
` Situation
y A large telecommunication company
y FaultsÆ alarms generated by network resources
e.g. devices are out of order or traffic bursts entail problems
y System operator tries to identify underlying faults: problematic since
the data stream of alarms is quite complex and dense
y Furthermore: events are imprecise wrt. time (no access to precision)
The Problem
` Aim
y automise identification of faults
y Intermediate aim: cluster alarmsÆ simplification for operator
Supporting network operators in localising faults
Reduction of information by generating high-level alarms
Reduction of workload for the operator
System Overview
• Focus on pattern representation

Method
` Relational system as a mean for representing termporal
information, mainly based on
y Allen 1983, 1984
y Vilain, Kautz 1986
y Ladkin, Maddux 1994
y Cohn, Hazarika 2001
Method
` Coarse set of jointly exhaustive and pairwise disjoints
relations between events (e.g. alarms)
` Relation algebraÆ CSP (e.g. for testing the consistency
of pattern descriptions or to complete knowledge)
An example: composing
relations
` Given three temporal entities: x, y, and z
` It holds: x > y and y c z
` The composition provides x > z

Summary
` Method
y A temporal (i.e. 1-dimensional) representation of events has been
proposed (including points and intervals in time)
y It can be dealt with using CSP techniques
` Application
y Thrown alarms in telecommunication networks: mining for frequent
patterns (mining for important patternsÆ expert knowledge)
y The generality of the approach allows the application in other
domains in the future
